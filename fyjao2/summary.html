Welcome to my first online portfolio! My name is Faye (Yueh) and I love creating things.

Although I wouldn't really label myself a gamer, I enjoy playing games. I've started playing video games as early on as when I started piano (i.e. 4 years old), and even though I don't have as much time to play nowadays, I still like games. Aside from video games, I also enjoyed playing music, drawing, and coming up with stories and characters. Recently, I've been trying to get into baking and making desserts. Ironically, I don't enjoy computer hardware and building parts as much as I should, being a student in the ICS department, but it is no doubt that I love creating things in general.

When I was little, my first dream job was to become an inventor, but all I had in the house was paper and tape lying around. As I was growing up, my dream professions changed from artist to musician, musician to author... During late elementary, I made several incomplete games. My old time favorite was a Mario Party clone made of paper, cardboard pieces, sharpies, and a lot of tape. Eventually, I resorted to using PowerPoint slideshows with button events as basic games.

But I could only do so much with PowerPoint, so I asked my tech-savvy father if he could teach me programming. It was a foreign word at the time, but I knew that it's what made games happen. He asked me why, and I explained that I couldn't allow text inputs in my PowerPoint games, as well as make numerous combinations of choices. When I was in middle school, my dad gave me a few lessons in Visual Basic (either 6 or .NET), and bought me my very own programming book.

Over the next few years, on top of my newfound programming knowledge, I continued to do hobbyist art, storywriting, and music-composition (mind you, they were mostly incomplete). I was never good at anything in particular, and it often discouraged me about my future. In high school, while my peers talked about their future career choices, I could only listen; I didn't even want to go to college because I didn't know what I want to do in life.

I applied to UC Irvine in the fall of 2011, and got accepted as a Computer Science major. After attending an info session at ICS Scholars Day, I've come to find that game development is actually a thing, a legitimate job. I began to pursue my newfound dream...

For more about my published work, feel free to visit the Work tab. To contact me, please see the Contact tab. Thank you!

~Faye Jao
